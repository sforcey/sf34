•The rate of growth and period of oscillation of the width of the dendritic crystal arm appear to be roughly the same as the corresponding numbers for the radius of  the dendrite.
•This seems to be related to the self similarity of the crystal.
•
•The authors describe multiple possible physical mechanisms for the out of phase growth pulsations.
•They theorize in general that the pulsations are strongly connected to the geometry of the  crystal, and therefore probably arise in other side-branching crystals, such as snowflakes.
